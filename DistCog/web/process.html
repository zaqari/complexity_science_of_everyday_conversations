Figure 1: An example of the steps involved in our analysis. The utterances in figure (a) are taken from the processed, aligned corpus, where each utterance $x$ is compared to a set of subsequent utterances $y$ up to 200 turns apart from one another. Utterances are numbered according to how many utterances after after the first, subsequent utterance $y$ spoken by the same speaker it is ($\Delta_t$). The utterance $x$ is compared to each utterance $y$ separately in order to calculate convergence information (CI) for each comparison. The bars in figure (b) capture CI for the utterance $x$ (printed on the y-axis) compared to subsequent utterances up until the end of a conversation. Figure (3) demonstrates Allan Variance for CI as a function of how many turns after the first subsequent utterance $y$ subsequent utterances are are for a total conversation, for the same speaker who wrote the utterances $x$ (in gold) and one of their partners (in blue).
